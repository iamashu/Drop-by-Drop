          Fog Drip Irrigation- Click to enlarge

The ends of the jute ropes are connected with the roots of the trees. When the fog occurs, the moist jute ropes will absorb the water from the air and the dew will condense on the ropes. When the ropes will get saturated, the ropes will start capillary action. This water from the ropes will go near the roots.           Macro Catchment Area

          Macro Catchment Area- Click To Enlarge
          In a drought prone area the soil’s quality of water retention is poor. So, the water which falls in the form of rain percolates through the soil. This results in water scarcity. To overcome this problem we have come up with an idea of macro catchments area. In this system we plant some trees in the drought prone area or take some existing trees. We make the land near these trees elevated and slanting towards the roots of the trees. Whenever there is rainfall the water from the regions near the trees will accommodate the area near the roots of the trees as the ground level is low here. This water is then absorbed by the roots of the trees. This avoids the water to percolate through the soil. This helps the tree to survive in these extreme conditions. This in turn helps to improve the quality of soil. The roots and the leaves shed by the trees helps to enrich the soil & by these we can convert an arid region into water-collecting land. We can also put straws and leaves to reduce evaporation.

          Surface Run Off Catchment Pit

          Arun placing container to store water
          Water getting collected in the pit
          Water getting purified by the pit
          Clean water getting collected in the container.
          Most of the water drains into the water bodies in the mountainous regions. So, the water which drains out gets wasted. But it can be used efficiently if we take such measures like as the mountains in India are barriers to the mountains the water should be collected to ensure the minimum wastage. We can make a concrete pit on the ground surface and we can connect some pipes to the pits so that the water which is collected in the pit during rain can be purified and moved to the tanks and the water which is collected in the tank can be used to supply the water to the homes so that that purified tank water can be used by the for the domestic purpose by the people of that area in the harsh conditions like drought. So these catch pits can be used to conserve water. When the rain falls on the hilly areas it can be stored in the catch pit and the water which is there in the catch pit can be used for industrial and agricultural purposes. The left out water can be purified and carried to the tanks to supply it to homes. So this way we can conserve water.

          Kitchen Waste Water Recycling

          Kitchen Waste water Recycling - Click to enlarge
          Recycling grey-water in kitchen is a good way. In kitchen we use detergents. It contain high amount of phosphorous. This can facilitate plant growth. But high amount of other dioxins can retard plant growth. So, we have devised an eco-friendly and economical way to recycle water.Water from sinks will go into the tank through the pipe. This tank will have fishes and plants like:
          Plants:
          At water-depth zone from 0-20cm; Iris pseudacorus, Sparganium erectum
          At water-depth zone from 40-60cm; Stratiotes aloides, Hydrocharis morsus-ranae
          At water-depth zone from 60-120cm; Nymphea alba, Nuphar lutea
          At submerged water-depth zone; Myriophyllum spicatum
          Fishes:
          Surface swimming fish: Leuciscus leuciscus, Scardinius erythrophthalmus
          Middle-swimmers: Rutilus rutilus
          Bottom-swimming fish: Tinca tinca
          This list is optional. You can choose 2-4 from them. Size of the tank should be 100 cm x 100 cm x 50 cm. When water will purify, it would go under an ion exchange membrane. It is commonly found in water softeners.  Ion exchange systems use ion exchange resin- or zeolite-packed columns to replace unwanted ions. The most common case is water softening consisting of removal of Ca2+ and Mg2+ ions replacing them with soap friendly Na+ or K+ ions. Ion exchange resins are also used to remove toxic ions such as nitrate, nitrite, lead, mercury, arsenic and many others.

          Infrastructural Solutions

          Individual Premises

          Individual Premises - Click to enlarge
          We suggest that premises should leave 25% open area as unpaved where they should make absorbing lawns with rocks, grass and sprinklers. For a plot of 100 sq.m. area,
          Open unpaved area : 25 sq.m.
          Rainfall received in the total area of the plot = 100 x 0.70  = 70 cu.m.
          All the open area and the rooftop should be able to hold the water upto the depth of 0.20 m maximum, keeping consideration of peak hours.
          So we propose-
          1. Rooftops should be made of RCC to hold water upto the height of 20 cm. 3" diameter pipe should be taken down to drain water. 
          We can connect it to 1.5" pipe to make sprinklers in the lawn. It will save the grass from being damaged.
          2. Open unpaved area should be 40 cm down from the floor level.
          An absorbing lawn should be made as per the details shown.

          More Water saving tips

          1.       Ships should have water harvesting system. In seas, there is heavy precipitation. This water can be used in pools or for bathing purpose. Sloping should be made on the top of the roof and this run-off can be collected in a recharge pit. Diatomaceous earth can be used in filters and this water can be used in showers and swimming pools.
          2.       If water runs off your lawn easily, split your watering time into shorter periods to allow for better absorption.
          3.       Shorten your shower by a minute or two and you'll save up to 150 gallons per month.
          4.       Put food coloring in your toilet tank. If it seeps into the toilet bowl without flushing, you have a leak. Fixing it can save up to 1,000 gallons a month.
          5.       Use dual-flush systems in your house. These flush use water for two types of waste – 3 litres for liquid waste and 6 litres for solid waste. Old single flush systems use 6 litres of water for every type of waste.
          6.       Use a minimum amount of organic or slow release fertilizer to promote a healthy and drought tolerant landscape.
          7.       Direct water from rain gutters and HVAC systems toward water-loving plants in the landscape for automatic water savings.
          8.       Use sprinklers that deliver big drops of water close to the ground. Smaller water drops and mist often evaporate before they hit the ground.
          9.       When washing dishes by hand, fill the sink basin or a large container and rinse when all of the dishes have been soaped and scrubbed.
          10.   If you accidentally drop ice cubes when filling your glass from the freezer, don't throw them in the sink. Drop them in a house plant instead.
